ARTICLE 1: STAMPEDE RCT (Radiotherapy in Metastatic Prostate Cancer)
Published: Lancet 2018; 392: 2353-66

PRIMARY FINDING:
  Radiotherapy does NOT improve overall survival in UNSELECTED metastatic prostate cancer
  HR 0.92 (95% CI 0.80-1.06), p=0.266
  Location: Results lines 558-576

CRITICAL SUBGROUP FINDING:
  ✓ LOW metastatic burden: BENEFITED from radiotherapy
    HR 0.68 (95% CI 0.52-0.90), p=0.007
    3-year OS: 73% vs 81% (8% absolute difference)
    
  ✗ HIGH metastatic burden: NO benefit
    HR 1.07 (95% CI 0.90-1.28), p=0.420
    3-year OS: 54% vs 53% (similar)
  
  INTERACTION p=0.0098 → Treatment effect DIFFERS significantly by burden
  Location: Results lines 570-580, Table 2, Figure 4A

SECONDARY FINDING (Interesting contrast):
  Failure-free survival IMPROVED overall:
  HR 0.76 (95% CI 0.68-0.84), p<0.0001
  But did NOT translate to OS survival benefit
  Location: Results lines 582-650

DESIGN FEATURES TO KNOW:
  - Stratified randomization: 7 factors (hospital, age, nodal status, PS, planned ADT/docetaxel, aspirin)
  - Sample size: 2061 (1029 control, 1032 radiotherapy)
  - Primary outcome: Overall survival
  - Power: 90% to detect HR 0.75
  - Analysis: Stratified log-rank test, Cox proportional hazards
  - Follow-up: 37 months median
  Location: Methods lines 21-30, 150-158, 345-350, 402-432

BASELINE CHARACTERISTICS (confirms randomization worked):
  - Median age: 68 years (IQR 63-73) - SAME in both arms
  - Median PSA: 97 ng/mL - SAME in both arms
  - 71% WHO performance status 0 - SAME in both arms
  - 64% nodal involvement (N+) - SAME in both arms
  - Table 1 shows groups well-balanced
  Location: Lines 517-556, Table 1

RADIOTHERAPY DOSE (compare with standard):
  - Daily schedule: 55 Gy in 20 fractions over 4 weeks (52% of patients)
  - Weekly schedule: 36 Gy in 6 fractions over 6 weeks (48% of patients)
  - Daily showed stronger benefit on FFS (HR 0.69 vs 0.85 weekly)
  Location: Lines 31-32, 200-204, 536-550

ADVERSE EVENTS - Well tolerated:
  - 5% RTOG grade 3-4 during radiotherapy
  - 4% RTOG grade 3-4 after radiotherapy
  - 38-39% any CTCAE grade 3+ event (similar between arms)
  Location: Lines 51-55

WHAT THIS MEANS:
  → Radiotherapy NOT recommended for all metastatic prostate cancer patients
  → May be considered for low metastatic burden subset
  → Improved biochemical control but not survival benefit in unselected patients

ARTICLE 2: NURSES' HEALTH STUDY (Rotating Night Shift Work & Type 2 Diabetes)
Published: BMJ 2018; 363: k4641

PRIMARY FINDING:
  Both rotating night shift work AND unhealthy lifestyle independently increase T2DM risk
  
  Shift work alone (per 5-year increment):
    HR 1.31 (95% CI 1.19-1.44) - modest effect
  
  Unhealthy lifestyle (per factor):
    HR 2.30 (1.88-2.83) - stronger effect
  
  Location: Abstract + Results Table 2, lines 320-350

CRITICAL INTERACTION FINDING:
  When BOTH shift work + unhealthy lifestyle present:
  
  Joint HR: 2.83 (95% CI 2.15-3.73)
  
  Multiplicative interaction: p > 0.67 (NOT significant)
    → Effects do NOT multiply on HR scale
  
  ADDITIVE interaction (RERI): p < 0.001 (SIGNIFICANT)
    → Effects DO add on risk difference scale
    → Joint risk exceeds independent sum
  
  Location: Results lines 413-437, Table 3

ATTRIBUTABLE PROPORTIONS (breakdown of joint risk):
  Among women with BOTH exposures:
  
    71.2% attributable to unhealthy lifestyle ALONE
    17.1% attributable to shift work ALONE
    11.3% attributable to their ADDITIVE INTERACTION
    ─────────
    100% total
  
  PUBLIC HEALTH IMPLICATION:
    - Modify lifestyle → prevent 71% of T2DM cases
    - Extra 11% benefit if shift workers adopt healthy lifestyle
    - Shift workers benefit MORE from lifestyle intervention
  
  Location: Table 3, lines 431-435

HIGHEST RISK GROUP:
  ≥10 years rotating night shift work + ≥3 unhealthy lifestyle factors
  HR 7.04 (95% CI 5.29-9.37)
  ~7-fold elevated risk vs reference group
  Location: Lines 413-421

STUDY DESIGN:
  - Type: Prospective cohort study (2 cohorts)
  - NHS: 55,324 women, baseline 1988, 22-24 years follow-up
  - NHS II: 88,086 women, baseline 1991, 22-24 years follow-up
  - Total participants: 143,410
  - Total T2DM cases: 10,915 (5,474 NHS + 5,441 NHS II)
  Location: Methods lines 122-145, Results lines 260-277

EXPOSURE DEFINITIONS:
  Rotating night shift work:
    ≥3 night shifts/month PLUS day/evening shifts same month
    Duration categories: Never, 1-5, 5-9, ≥10 years
  
  Unhealthy lifestyle (4 INDEPENDENT factors):
    1. Current smoking
    2. Physical activity <30 min/day at moderate-vigorous intensity
    3. Diet quality in bottom 3/5 of Alternate Healthy Eating Index
    4. BMI ≥25 kg/m²
    Scored 0-4 scale (sum of factors present)
  
  Location: Methods lines 101-116, 119-166, Abstract

OUTCOME VALIDATION (Why 98% concordance important):
  Self-reported T2DM on questionnaire
  ↓
  Supplementary questionnaire with diagnostic criteria
  ↓
  Investigator review (standardized)
  ↓
  Validation check: 98% concordance with blinded medical record review
  
  Diagnostic criteria applied:
    Pre-1998: NDDG (classic symptoms + glucose OR ≥2 glucose readings OR treatment)
    Post-1998: ADA (fasting glucose ≥7.0 mmol/L)
  
  Location: Methods lines 200-225

STATISTICAL APPROACH:
  - Analyzed separately per cohort (different ages, baseline years)
  - Then pooled with inverse-variance weighted random effects meta-analysis
  - Used multivariable time-dependent Cox models (covariates updated biennially)
  - Tested BOTH multiplicative AND additive interaction (rare!)
  - Decomposed joint effect into proportional contributions
  - 5 sensitivity analyses to confirm robustness
  
  Location: Methods lines 227-277, 250-258

COHORT DIFFERENCES (Age effect):
  NHS (older, mean age 55): RERI 0.13
  NHS II (younger, mean age 36): RERI 0.32 ← 2.5x stronger interaction
  
  Interpretation: Younger women show stronger synergistic effects of shift work + lifestyle
  
  Location: Results lines 436-437, Discussion lines 489-550

SENSITIVITY ANALYSES (shows robustness):
  All effects remained significant when:
  - Adjusted for hypertension/hyperlipidemia
  - Restricted to symptomatic diabetes only
  - Used baseline (not updated) lifestyle factors
  - Used weighted (vs equal-weight) lifestyle score
  - Removed BMI from lifestyle definition
  
  Location: Results lines 438-456

WHAT THIS MEANS:
  → Lifestyle intervention most important for preventing T2DM
  → Shift workers get additional benefit from lifestyle changes (11% synergistic)
  → Focus on modifiable factors (smoking, activity, diet, weight)
  → Age affects magnitude of interaction (younger benefit more)

COMPARING THE TWO STUDIES - Key Contrasts

| Feature | RC (STAMPEDE) | EPI (Nurses' Health) |
|---------|---------------|----------------------|
| Q being answered | Does intervention work? | Do exposures associate? |
| Design strength for causation | HIGH (randomization) | MEDIUM (observational) |
| Sample size | 2,061 | 143,410 |
| Follow-up | 37 months | 22-24 years |
| Main outcome | Hard (death) | Soft (self-reported disease) |
| Primary question | Overall survival | Joint associations |
| Randomization | Yes, stratified (7 factors) | No, observational |
| Confounder control | Eliminated by randomization | Adjusted multivariable models |
| Interaction testing | Prespecified subgroup analysis | Multiplicative + additive |
| Main statistical model | Stratified log-rank test, Cox | Cox with time-varying covariates |
| Effect modifier tested | Metastatic burden (prespecified) | Multiplicative & additive scales |
| Main finding | No OS benefit (unselected); yes in low burden | Additive interaction; 71% lifestyle |
| Clinical implication | Radiotherapy only for low burden | Lifestyle modification priority |

MOST LIKELY EXAM QUESTIONS

1. "What was the main finding of STAMPEDE?"
   → No OS benefit for unselected patients; subgroup benefit in low burden (HR 0.68, p=0.007)
   → Location: Results lines 558-576, 570-580

2. "Explain the subgroup interaction in STAMPEDE."
   → Treatment effect differs significantly by metastatic burden (p=0.0098)
   → Low burden: HR 0.68 (benefit); High burden: HR 1.07 (no benefit)
   → Location: Table 2, Figure 4A

3. "What was the main finding of the Nurses' Health study?"
   → Additive interaction between shift work and unhealthy lifestyle (p<0.001)
   → Joint HR 2.83; attributable proportions 71% lifestyle, 11% interaction
   → Location: Results lines 413-437, Table 3

4. "Explain multiplicative vs additive interaction in the Nurses' study."
   → Multiplicative p>0.67 (not sig) = effects don't multiply
   → Additive p<0.001 (sig) = effects add on risk difference scale
   → Location: Results lines 413-437, Methods lines 210-214

5. "What does the 71% attributable proportion mean?"
   → 71% of T2DM in shift workers with unhealthy lifestyle is due to lifestyle alone
   → Public health implication: modify lifestyle to prevent most cases
   → Location: Table 3, Discussion

6. "Why did STAMPEDE show improved FFS but not OS?"
   → FFS is early event (months); OS is delayed (years)
   → 37-month follow-up may be insufficient
   → Radiotherapy delays but may not prevent death
   → Location: Results lines 582-650

7. "What stratification factors were used in STAMPEDE?"
   → Hospital, age, nodal status, WHO PS, planned ADT, planned docetaxel, aspirin/NSAID use
   → Location: Methods lines 150-158, Table 1 confirms balance

8. "How was diabetes outcome validated in Nurses' study?"
   → Self-report + supplementary questionnaire + investigator review + 98% medical record concordance
   → Location: Methods lines 200-225

9. "What is intention-to-treat analysis and why important?"
   → Analyze in allocated arm regardless of adherence (preserves randomization benefit)
   → Location: Methods lines 502-503

10. "Why analyze NHS and NHS II separately then pool?"
    → Different baseline years, ages, questionnaires → different confounding structures
    → Separate analysis controls better; pooling gives overall estimate
    → Location: Methods lines 250-258

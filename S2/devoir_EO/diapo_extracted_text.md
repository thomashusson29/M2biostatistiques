## Notes 3

- Selective dopamine D2/D3 receptor agonist Distinct from standard antidepressants which target serotonin Interest in depression :    - Pramipexole is a  selective dopamine D2/D3 receptor agonist   - It is pharmacologically distinct from standard antidepressants, which mainly     target  serotonin   - It is of interest in depression because it may target  anhedonia ,  low      motivation , and  reward-processing deficits   - The biological rationale is that dopaminergic signalling is involved in      reward learning  and motivational drive   - In this study, pramipexole was used as an  add-on treatment  to ongoing     antidepressant therapy   Et pour la slide  suivante  :    Evidence Base for Pramipexole in Depression   - Before this trial, evidence was  promising but limited   - The article cites a  systematic review  reporting:   -  5 completed randomized controlled trials   -  3 open-label studies   -  5 observational studies   - One prior RCT in  65 unipolar TRD patients  found benefit on a continuous     depression outcome, but not clearly on response   - Another RCT suggested that  1 mg/day  could be effective, while  5 mg/day  had     poor tolerability   - A very small trial in unipolar depression did  not  show a clear between-group     difference   - Case series suggested possible benefit at  higher doses , around  2.5 mg/day   - Therefore, before the current paper, the signal was  interesting but not      definitive   - The current Lancet Psychiatry trial provides the  strongest randomized      evidence so far  in treatment-resistant unipolar depression

## Notes 5

- Randomisation  was performed centrally through an online system,   with a 1:1 allocation ratio.   The authors used a  minimisation  procedure   to balance the two groups on four important prognostic factors:   study site, age, sex, and baseline depression severity.   The first ten participants were  randomised  without  minimisation   in order to avoid predictability at the beginning of the trial.   After that, the algorithm  favoured , with an 80% probability,   the treatment allocation that best reduced imbalance between the groups,   while still preserving a degree of randomness.   This strategy improved baseline comparability between the groups   and strengthened internal validity,   while also maintaining good allocation concealment.

## Notes 15

- Functional unblinding  likely: many participants correctly guessed treatment     allocation   - Primary outcome was  self-rated , so more vulnerable to expectancy bias   - Tolerability issue: more discontinuations due to adverse effects with     pramipexole   - Recognisable adverse-effect profile may have contributed to unmasking   - Moderate sample size: limited power for rare harms and subgroup analyses   - External validity remains limited: UK sites, selected TRD population,     several psychiatric exclusions   - No ethnicity data collected   - Long-term interpretation is weaker because follow-up at 48 weeks was     incomplete for many participants and antidepressant changes were allowed     after week 12
